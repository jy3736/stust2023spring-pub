  1. What are combinational logic circuits?

    A. Analog circuits that perform mathematical operations on one or more input signals to produce a single output signal B. Digital circuits that perform logical operations on one or more input signals to produce a single output signal C. Circuits that store information from previous inputs to produce a single output signal D. Circuits that only work with Boolean equations

    Answer: B

  1. Why are combinational logic circuits called "combinational"? A. Their output depends on previous inputs and internal memory B. Their output depends on the type of input signals used C.     Answer: C

    A. Transistors, resistors, capacitors, and inductors B. Power supplies, amplifiers, and filters C. Adders, decoders, multiplexers, and comparators D. None of the above

    Answer: C
